Colorado Springs Mold Inspection — Know What You're Dealing With

Mold inspection is the first step every homeowner should take when they suspect a problem. Without accurate detection, you're either over-treating a small issue or missing a larger one that will cost significantly more to fix later. In Colorado Springs, dry air creates a false sense of security — but rapid humidity swings push moisture into basements and crawl spaces where mold grows unseen for months.

When You Need a Mold Inspection

Don't wait until mold is extensive and visible. These are the signals that warrant an inspection now:

  • Musty or earthy odors in the basement, crawl space, or near HVAC vents
  • Any plumbing leak, roof leak, or flood event — even one that was "cleaned up"
  • Recurring dark spots on walls, ceilings, or grout that keep coming back after cleaning
  • Family members with persistent unexplained allergy symptoms, headaches, or respiratory issues
  • Condensation on windows or walls that persists through winter and spring
  • Buying or selling a home — mold that standard inspections miss can become a significant liability

Colorado Springs' freeze-thaw cycles and monsoon moisture events frequently push water into basements and crawl spaces. By the time you see or smell mold, the colony is often well-established and larger than what's visible.

How the Inspection Works

  1. Visual Assessment: Walk-through of all accessible areas — attic, basement, crawlspace, bathrooms, kitchen, laundry, HVAC. We document visible mold, water staining, and intrusion signs with photos.
  2. Moisture Mapping: Calibrated moisture meters identify elevated readings in walls, floors, and ceilings — revealing hidden moisture and active leaks before damage is visible.
  3. Source Identification: We locate the moisture source. Common culprits in this area: snowmelt infiltration, exhaust fans venting into attics, crawlspace vapor migration, condensate drain issues.
  4. Air Quality Sampling (if warranted): Surface swabs or air cassettes sent to an AIHA-accredited lab. Results identify mold species and spore counts compared to outdoor baseline.
  5. Written Report: Findings, photos, moisture readings, lab results (if applicable), and prioritized recommendations within 48 hours.

What Every Inspection Includes

  • Full visual walk-through of all accessible areas
  • Calibrated moisture meter readings in all suspect areas
  • Identification of moisture source(s)
  • Written findings report with photos
  • Honest itemized remediation quote — no obligation to proceed

What Does Mold Inspection Cost?

Visual inspections are free. Air quality testing with lab analysis runs $150–$400 depending on the number of samples. A full inspection with lab report typically runs $300–$600. Remediation quotes are always included at no charge. You'll know what you're dealing with and what it'll cost to fix before committing to anything.

Is mold inspection covered by insurance?

Typically not as a standalone service. When mold inspection is part of documenting a covered water event (burst pipe, storm), some policies cover related costs. We provide documentation to support insurance claims where applicable.

How long does an inspection take?

Most residential inspections take 1–2 hours. Larger homes with finished basements, attic access, and crawlspaces may take up to 3 hours. Lab results, when ordered, return in 3–5 business days.
