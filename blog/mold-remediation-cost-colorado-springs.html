How Much Does Mold Remediation Cost in Colorado Springs? [2025]

A straightforward pricing guide for Colorado Springs homeowners — from small bathroom mold to full basement remediation.

Home › Blog › Mold Remediation Cost Colorado Springs

If you've discovered mold in your Colorado Springs home, one of the first questions on your mind is: how much is this going to cost? The honest answer is that it depends significantly on how much mold you have, where it is, and what materials are affected. This guide breaks down real pricing data for 2025 so you know what to expect.

Colorado Springs Mold Remediation Cost — By Project Size

Small Mold Problems (Under 10 sq ft) — $500 to $1,500

A single bathroom with grout mold, a small patch of drywall after a minor leak, or surface mold on bathroom caulk falls in this range. These projects often take a half-day to one day. The cost covers containment setup, physical removal of affected material, antimicrobial treatment, and HEPA vacuuming. Clearance testing is optional but recommended.

Medium Projects (10–50 sq ft, 1–2 Rooms) — $1,500 to $4,000

This is the most common residential mold remediation scope in Colorado Springs. A bathroom with mold behind the walls, a bedroom corner with hidden water intrusion, or a crawlspace with limited mold coverage. Costs include full containment, drywall removal and disposal, structural drying if needed, antimicrobial treatment, and reconstruction prep.

Basement or Crawlspace Remediation — $3,000 to $8,000+

Basements and crawlspaces account for the majority of serious mold remediation cases in Colorado Springs, thanks to snowmelt infiltration, inadequate vapor barriers, and drainage issues. The larger the affected area and the more structural material involved (framing, subfloor), the higher the cost. Full crawlspace encapsulation can add $2,000–$5,000 on top of remediation but prevents recurrence.

Severe or Whole-House Contamination — $10,000 to $30,000+

Large-scale mold resulting from flood damage, long-term hidden leaks, or HVAC system contamination that has distributed spores throughout the home requires the most extensive remediation. These projects often involve structural material removal across multiple rooms, full HVAC cleaning, and extended drying timelines.

What Drives Mold Remediation Cost in Colorado Springs

  • Affected area size: More square footage means more labor, materials, and disposal costs.
  • Material type: Mold in drywall is cheaper to remediate than mold in structural framing or HVAC systems.
  • Accessibility: Crawlspaces, attic spaces, and behind finished walls cost more to access and treat.
  • Mold species: Black mold (Stachybotrys) and other toxic species may require additional safety protocols.
  • Reconstruction: After drywall removal, rebuilding the affected area is a separate cost — typically $15–$30/sq ft.
  • Moisture source repair: Fixing the plumbing leak, roof penetration, or foundation issue causing the mold isn't included in remediation — but it's essential.

Colorado-Specific Cost Factors

Colorado Springs has some unique factors that can affect your remediation cost compared to national averages:

  • High-altitude drying rates: Colorado's low humidity and high altitude actually speed up structural drying, which can reduce drying time and cost compared to humid climates.
  • Freeze-thaw damage: Colorado's dramatic temperature swings frequently cause water infiltration through building envelopes, creating mold in locations not typical in other climates — like around exterior walls at grade level.
  • Crawlspace prevalence: Many Colorado Springs homes were built with crawlspaces rather than full basements, which are a common mold hotspot due to soil moisture and inadequate vapor barriers.

3 Frequently Asked Questions About Mold Remediation Cost

What is the average cost of mold remediation in Colorado Springs?

Most residential projects cost $1,500–$5,000. Small problems under 10 sq ft run $500–$1,500. Basement or crawlspace remediation runs $3,000–$8,000+. Severe whole-house contamination can exceed $20,000. All estimates from Springs Mold Solutions are free and include a detailed scope of work.

Is mold remediation covered by homeowner's insurance in Colorado?

Coverage depends on cause. Sudden accidental water damage (burst pipe, appliance failure) is usually covered, including mold if addressed promptly. Gradual leaks and outside flooding are typically excluded. Document everything and call your insurer before starting cleanup.

How do I know if I need remediation or just cleaning?

Mold on non-porous surfaces (tile grout, sealed counters) can sometimes be cleaned. Mold on porous materials (drywall, wood, insulation) requires remediation — surface cleaning doesn't kill mold roots. Any affected area over 10 sq ft, or mold in HVAC systems, requires professional remediation regardless of surface type.
