7 Signs You Have Mold in Your Colorado Springs Home

Published February 23, 2026 · By Springs Mold Solutions

Mold doesn't always announce itself with obvious green or black patches on the wall. In many Colorado Springs homes, mold hides behind drywall, under flooring, and inside air ducts — growing silently for months before homeowners notice anything wrong. Here are the seven most common signs that you may have a mold problem in your home.

1. A Persistent Musty Smell

This is the most common first clue. If you notice a damp, earthy, or musty odor — especially in your basement, bathroom, or crawl space — there's a good chance mold is growing somewhere nearby. The smell comes from microbial volatile organic compounds (MVOCs) that mold releases as it feeds on organic materials.

Pay attention to rooms where the smell is stronger. Even if you can't see mold, the odor often points directly to the source. Many homeowners in older neighborhoods like Old Colorado City report musty smells that turned out to be hidden mold behind plaster walls.

2. Visible Discoloration on Walls or Ceilings

Mold doesn't always look like the dramatic black patches you see online. It can appear as:

  • Small dark spots that look like dirt or soot
  • Yellowish or brownish stains on ceilings
  • White, thread-like growth on wood surfaces
  • Green or gray fuzzy patches in damp areas
  • Pink or orange film in bathrooms (often on grout or caulk)

If you see any discoloration in areas that tend to stay moist, don't dismiss it. What looks like a simple water stain could be mold growth underneath. Learn more about identifying different types of mold.

3. Water Stains or Past Water Damage

Anywhere water has intruded — past or present — is a prime location for mold. Look for:

  • Water rings on ceilings (from roof leaks or plumbing above)
  • Bubbling or peeling paint
  • Warped or buckled flooring
  • Stains around windows, especially after heavy rain or snowmelt

Colorado Springs homeowners often see water intrusion during spring snowmelt, particularly in neighborhoods at higher elevations like Briargate and Monument. Even if the water damage happened months ago, mold can still be growing if the area wasn't properly dried. Our water damage restoration service addresses both the moisture and any resulting mold.

4. Unexplained Allergic Reactions or Health Symptoms

Mold exposure can cause a range of health symptoms, many of which mimic seasonal allergies. Watch for:

  • Sneezing, runny nose, or nasal congestion that won't go away
  • Itchy, watery eyes
  • Persistent cough or throat irritation
  • Skin rashes or irritation
  • Headaches that seem worse at home
  • Worsening asthma symptoms

The key indicator: symptoms improve when you leave the house and return when you come home. If multiple family members are experiencing similar issues, mold should be high on your list of suspects.

5. Condensation on Windows

Excessive condensation on your windows — especially during Colorado's cold winter months — indicates high indoor humidity. When moisture consistently forms on glass surfaces, it often condenses on hidden surfaces too: inside walls, behind furniture, and in poorly ventilated areas.

Colorado Springs sits at 6,035 feet elevation where outdoor air is naturally dry, but indoor humidity can spike from cooking, showering, and poor ventilation. If your windows regularly fog up, check nearby walls and window sills for early signs of mold. Read our guide on preventing mold at Colorado Springs altitude.

6. Peeling Wallpaper or Bubbling Paint

When wallpaper starts peeling at the edges or paint begins to bubble and crack, moisture is usually the culprit. That moisture creates the perfect conditions for mold to grow behind the surface — where you can't see it.

This is especially common in bathrooms without exhaust fans, kitchens with poor ventilation, and basement walls that aren't properly waterproofed. Don't just repaint or re-wallpaper — address the underlying moisture issue first, or the mold will come right back.

7. Your Home Has a History of Flooding or Leaks

If your home has ever experienced flooding, a significant plumbing leak, or a roof leak, there's a higher-than-average chance that mold developed as a result — especially if the water damage wasn't professionally remediated within 24–48 hours.

Mold can begin growing within 24 hours of water exposure and can spread rapidly behind walls and under flooring. If you bought a home with a history of water damage, or if you had a flood that was cleaned up without professional drying and testing, a professional mold inspection is a smart investment.

What to Do If You Spot These Signs

If you've noticed one or more of these signs in your home, here's what we recommend:

  1. Don't disturb the area. Scrubbing visible mold without proper containment can release thousands of spores into the air.
  2. Reduce moisture. Fix any active leaks, run dehumidifiers, and improve ventilation.
  3. Get a professional inspection. A trained inspector can identify hidden mold, determine the source, and recommend the right remediation approach.
