How to Prevent Mold at Colorado Springs' Altitude

Practical prevention tips for homes at 6,035 feet

Colorado Springs sits at 6,035 feet — and while our dry climate might seem like it would prevent mold, the reality is more complicated. Altitude creates unique conditions that can actually encourage mold growth if you're not careful.

Why High Altitude Doesn't Mean Mold-Free

Many Colorado Springs homeowners assume our dry air eliminates mold risk. But our climate creates specific moisture pockets that mold loves:

  • Extreme temperature swings — 40-degree daily shifts cause condensation on windows, pipes, and exterior walls
  • Forced heating 8+ months — furnaces dry the air but push moisture into wall cavities
  • Snowmelt intrusion — spring runoff finds foundation cracks you didn't know existed
  • Humidifiers overcompensating — many homeowners run humidifiers to combat dry air, accidentally creating ideal mold conditions

Room-by-Room Prevention

Bathrooms

Run exhaust fans for 20 minutes after every shower. At our altitude, moisture evaporates faster from skin but lingers on cold tile and grout. Check caulking around tubs annually — our freeze-thaw cycles crack sealant faster than sea-level climates.

Basement & Crawl Spaces

Colorado Springs has expansive clay soil that retains moisture. Use a dehumidifier to keep basement humidity below 50%. Inspect foundation walls after heavy rain or snowmelt — water intrusion is the #1 cause of basement mold in our area.

Kitchen

Always use the range hood when cooking. Steam from boiling water rises and condenses on cold surfaces. Check under sinks monthly — slow leaks in our hard water environment cause mineral buildup that traps moisture.

HVAC System

Replace filters every 60-90 days (not 90-120 like sea-level recommendations). Our furnaces run harder and longer. Have ducts inspected every 2-3 years — condensation inside ductwork is invisible but common at altitude.

The Humidity Sweet Spot

Keep indoor humidity between 30-50%. Below 30% and you get cracked wood, static, and respiratory issues. Above 50% and mold starts growing. A $20 hygrometer from any hardware store is your best friend. Place one in each bathroom and the basement.

When Prevention Isn't Enough

If you spot mold despite your best efforts, don't panic — but don't ignore it either. Small patches (under 10 square feet) can sometimes be handled with proper cleaning. Anything larger, or mold that keeps returning, means there's an underlying moisture source that needs professional diagnosis.
