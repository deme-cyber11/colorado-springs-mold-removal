Does Homeowners Insurance Cover Mold Removal?

Published February 23, 2026 · By Springs Mold Solutions

Discovering mold in your home is stressful enough without worrying about the cost. One of the first questions most Colorado Springs homeowners ask us is: "Will my insurance cover this?" The answer isn't a simple yes or no — it depends entirely on what caused the mold and the specifics of your policy.

When Insurance Typically Covers Mold

Most standard homeowners insurance policies will cover mold damage when it's the result of a "covered peril" — an event that your policy already insures against. Common covered scenarios include:

Sudden Pipe Bursts or Plumbing Failures

If a pipe bursts unexpectedly and the resulting water damage leads to mold growth, your insurance will generally cover both the water damage restoration and the mold remediation. The key word is "sudden" — the damage can't have been building gradually.

Appliance Malfunctions

A washing machine hose that suddenly breaks, a water heater that fails, or a dishwasher that leaks unexpectedly — mold resulting from these sudden appliance failures is typically covered.

Storm or Weather Damage

If wind, hail, or a fallen tree damages your roof and water intrusion leads to mold, that's generally a covered peril. Colorado Springs sees its share of severe weather, especially during spring and summer storm season, making this a relatively common claim scenario.

Fire Suppression

If your home suffers fire damage and the water used to extinguish the fire leads to mold growth, the mold remediation is typically included in your fire damage claim.

When Insurance Usually Does NOT Cover Mold

Insurance companies deny mold claims more often than they approve them. Here are the most common exclusions:

Gradual Leaks and Long-Term Moisture

This is the biggest exclusion. If a pipe has been slowly leaking for months, if your roof has been deteriorating over time, or if poor grading has been directing water toward your foundation, the resulting mold is almost always excluded. Insurers consider this a maintenance issue, not a sudden event.

Humidity and Condensation

Mold caused by high indoor humidity, poor ventilation, or condensation is not covered. This is particularly relevant in Colorado Springs, where homeowners sometimes over-humidify during dry winter months. Our guide on preventing mold at altitude covers humidity management strategies.

Flooding

Standard homeowners insurance does not cover flood damage — you need a separate flood insurance policy for that. If your basement floods due to heavy rain or snowmelt and mold develops, your standard policy won't cover it unless you have flood insurance.

Neglect or Deferred Maintenance

If your insurer determines that the mold resulted from failure to maintain your property — not fixing a known leak, ignoring signs of water damage, or not maintaining your HVAC system — the claim will likely be denied.

Mold Coverage Limits in Colorado

Even when mold is covered, most policies include specific mold coverage limits:

  • Standard limit: $5,000–$10,000 for mold remediation
  • Enhanced coverage: Some policies offer $25,000–$50,000 mold endorsements for an additional premium
  • Sublimits: Your mold coverage may be a sublimit within your overall policy — meaning it comes out of your total coverage, not on top of it

Check your policy declarations page for specific mold coverage language. In Colorado, insurers are required to clearly state mold exclusions and limitations.

How to File a Mold Insurance Claim

If you believe your mold situation should be covered, here's the process we recommend:

Step 1: Document Everything

Take photos and videos of the mold and any related water damage before disturbing anything. Document the date you discovered the problem, the suspected cause, and any health symptoms your family has experienced.

Step 2: Contact Your Insurance Company Promptly

Report the claim as soon as possible. Delays in reporting can give insurers grounds to deny or reduce your claim. Most policies require "timely" notification.

Step 3: Get a Professional Inspection

A professional mold inspection with documented findings, air quality test results, and a clear assessment of the cause strengthens your claim significantly. At Springs Mold Solutions, we provide detailed inspection reports that many homeowners use for their insurance claims.

Step 4: Get Written Estimates

Before remediation begins, get a detailed written estimate. Your insurance company may want to send their own adjuster, and having a professional estimate gives you a baseline for comparison. Understanding typical mold removal costs in Colorado Springs helps you evaluate whether your insurer's offer is fair.

Step 5: Don't Wait to Start Mitigation

Most policies require you to take reasonable steps to prevent further damage. This means stopping the water source and beginning basic mitigation (like running fans and dehumidifiers) even before your claim is approved. Keep all receipts.

Tips for Maximizing Your Mold Claim

  • Read your policy carefully — know your mold coverage limits before you need them
  • Maintain your home — keep records of regular maintenance, plumbing inspections, and HVAC service
  • Act quickly — the faster you report and remediate, the stronger your claim
  • Use licensed professionals — insurers are more likely to honor claims remediated by licensed, certified companies
  • Consider a public adjuster — for large claims ($10,000+), a public adjuster can negotiate on your behalf
  • Don't accept the first offer — insurance companies often start low; a well-documented claim gives you leverage

What If Insurance Doesn't Cover It?

If your mold claim is denied, you still have options:

  • Appeal the decision — provide additional documentation or get a second opinion from an independent inspector
  • Contact the Colorado Division of Insurance — if you believe your claim was wrongfully denied
  • Explore financing — many remediation companies (including us) can discuss payment options for out-of-pocket projects

Regardless of insurance coverage, addressing mold promptly protects your family's health and prevents the problem from getting more expensive over time.
