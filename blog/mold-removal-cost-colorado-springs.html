How Much Does Mold Removal Cost in Colorado Springs? (2026 Guide)

Published February 23, 2026 · By Springs Mold Solutions

If you've discovered mold in your Colorado Springs home, one of the first questions on your mind is probably: how much is this going to cost? The answer depends on several factors, including the size of the affected area, the type of mold, and where it's growing. In this guide, we'll break down the real costs homeowners in the Pikes Peak region can expect in 2026.

Average Mold Removal Costs in Colorado Springs

Based on current market data and our own project history, here's what most Colorado Springs homeowners pay for professional mold remediation:

  • Small area (under 50 sq ft): $500–$1,500
  • Medium project (50–150 sq ft): $1,500–$4,000
  • Large remediation (150–300 sq ft): $4,000–$9,000
  • Whole-house or severe contamination: $9,000–$20,000+

The average homeowner in Colorado Springs spends between $1,500 and $5,000 for a typical mold removal project. That range covers most bathroom, basement, and crawl space jobs.

Factors That Affect the Price

1. Size of the Affected Area

This is the single biggest cost driver. A small patch of mold behind a bathroom vanity is a very different job than mold spread across an entire basement. Professional remediators typically price by the square foot, ranging from $10 to $25 per square foot for standard remediation.

2. Type of Mold

Black mold (Stachybotrys chartarum) requires additional safety protocols — full containment, specialized PPE, and HEPA air scrubbing. This can add 20–40% to the total cost compared to removing common molds like Cladosporium or Penicillium. Our black mold removal service follows strict IICRC guidelines for safe handling.

3. Location Within Your Home

Mold in easy-to-reach areas (like a bathroom wall) costs less than mold behind drywall, inside ductwork, or in a crawl space. Crawl space mold removal in Colorado Springs typically runs $1,500–$8,000 depending on the size and whether encapsulation is needed.

4. Extent of Structural Damage

If mold has damaged drywall, insulation, framing, or subflooring, those materials need to be removed and replaced. Structural repairs can add $1,000–$5,000 or more to the project, depending on what's affected.

5. Testing and Post-Remediation Verification

A professional mold inspection with air quality testing typically costs $300–$600. Post-remediation clearance testing (which we strongly recommend) adds another $200–$400. At Springs Mold Solutions, we include a free visual inspection with every estimate.

Colorado Springs–Specific Cost Factors

Colorado Springs has some unique factors that can influence mold removal costs:

  • Altitude and dry climate: While our low humidity generally means less mold than coastal cities, when mold does occur it's often in concentrated areas — basements, crawl spaces, and bathrooms where moisture gets trapped. Learn more about preventing mold at altitude.
  • Older homes: Many homes in neighborhoods like Old Colorado City and Manitou Springs have older plumbing and ventilation systems that create hidden moisture problems.
  • Seasonal snowmelt: Spring snowmelt can cause water intrusion in basements, particularly in areas like Briargate and Monument that sit at higher elevations.

DIY vs. Professional Mold Removal: Is It Worth the Savings?

For very small areas (under 10 square feet), the EPA says homeowners can attempt cleanup themselves using proper precautions. A DIY approach might cost $50–$200 in supplies.

However, professional remediation is strongly recommended when:

  • The affected area exceeds 10 square feet
  • Mold is inside walls, ductwork, or structural materials
  • You suspect black mold or toxic species
  • Anyone in the home has respiratory issues or allergies
  • Mold keeps coming back after cleaning

Improper removal can actually spread mold spores throughout your home, turning a $2,000 problem into a $10,000 one. Professional mold remediation includes containment, HEPA filtration, and clearance testing to ensure the job is done right.

Does Insurance Cover Mold Removal?

This is one of the most common questions we hear. The short answer: it depends on the cause. Most homeowners insurance policies cover mold if it resulted from a "covered peril" like a burst pipe, but not from gradual neglect or long-term moisture issues.
