What Affects the Cost?

  • Size of affected area — Larger areas require more labor, materials, and containment setup.
  • Type of mold — Toxic black mold requires additional safety measures and specialized treatment.
  • Location — Mold behind walls, under flooring, or in attics/crawl spaces costs more to access and remediate.
  • Structural damage — If mold has damaged drywall, framing, or insulation, replacement adds to cost.
  • Moisture source — Fixing the underlying moisture problem (plumbing repair, drainage, ventilation) is separate but essential.

⚠️ Important: Delaying mold removal always costs more. Mold spreads rapidly — what starts as a $500 job can become a $5,000+ project in a matter of weeks. Early intervention saves you money.

Insurance Coverage

Many homeowner's insurance policies cover mold remediation when the mold was caused by a covered event (like a burst pipe or storm damage). Mold from long-term maintenance issues is typically excluded. We work with all major insurance carriers and can help you document your claim. Read our FAQ for more details on insurance coverage.
