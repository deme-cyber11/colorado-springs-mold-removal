Mold Removal Pricing in Colorado Springs — Honest Numbers, No Surprises

Most mold remediation companies avoid putting prices on their website. We don't. Here's what mold removal actually costs in the Colorado Springs area, what drives the number up or down, and what's always included regardless of scope. The estimate is free — these ranges just help you come in with realistic expectations and avoid the sticker shock that leads to bad decisions.

What Does Mold Remediation Cost?

Mold inspection / testing: $0–$600
Visual inspections are free. Air quality testing or lab analysis (when needed to confirm species, extent, or clearance) runs $150–$400 depending on sample count. A full inspection with lab report typically runs $300–$600. You'll never pay for the initial visual inspection and quote.

Small remediation (under 10 sq ft): $500–$1,500
Isolated mold in a bathroom corner, under a sink, or in a single area of drywall. Typically a one-day job. Includes containment, removal of affected material, antimicrobial treatment, and cleanup. Most appropriate when the mold is localized and the moisture source is already identified and corrected.

Mid-size remediation (10–100 sq ft): $1,500–$4,000
The most common scenario — a basement wall section, bathroom with mold behind the tile, a crawl space joist area. Typically 1–2 days. This tier covers one room or one defined area. Most single-room jobs in Colorado Springs land here.

Large / whole-room remediation: $4,000–$9,000
Extensive contamination covering multiple rooms, significant wall cavity involvement, or basement flooding aftermath. Structural wood treatment, multi-day containment, and more material removal put jobs in this range. Active water damage events that weren't caught quickly often land here.

Crawl space mold and encapsulation: $2,500–$6,000
Crawl space mold is extremely common in El Paso County homes where monsoon moisture enters through vents and vapor migration. Remediation addresses the mold; encapsulation seals the crawl space to prevent recurrence. Many homeowners do both at once since the space is already open.

Post-remediation clearance testing: $300–$500
Third-party air sampling after remediation confirms mold levels are back to normal. We recommend independent testing — not our own — so results are unbiased. This is your written proof the job is done and is often required for insurance claims and home sales.

What Affects the Final Number

  • Size of the affected area — the primary driver. More square footage means more labor, materials, and disposal.
  • How accessible the mold is — mold inside wall cavities, under concrete slabs, or in tight crawl spaces requires more time and material removal than surface mold.
  • How long the moisture was present — a slow leak undetected for months creates far more contamination than a pipe that burst and was fixed within a day.
  • Type of affected materials — porous materials (drywall, insulation, wood trim) must be removed and replaced. Non-porous surfaces can be treated in place.
  • Whether structural wood is involved — joist and stud treatment or replacement adds to both labor and material cost.
  • HVAC involvement — mold in ductwork or air handlers requires specialized treatment and is priced separately based on system size.

What Every Job Includes — Regardless of Price

  • Written, itemized quote before any work begins — no vague estimates
  • Full containment with plastic sheeting and negative air pressure
  • HEPA vacuuming and EPA-registered antimicrobial treatment
  • Proper disposal of all removed contaminated materials
  • Full cleanup of the work area after completion
  • Moisture source identification and correction guidance
  • Written warranty on all remediated areas
  • Post-remediation clearance testing recommendation with independent lab

Pricing Questions We Get All the Time

Do you charge for estimates?

No. The estimate is free, comes with a written quote, and carries no obligation. We inspect, determine scope, and give you an itemized number before any work starts.

Can I get a ballpark over the phone?

We can give you a rough range based on your description, but mold jobs vary significantly once walls are open. The free on-site inspection is the only way to give you an accurate number — and it costs you nothing to find out.

What if the price changes after you start?

Our quotes are fixed for the agreed scope. The one exception: if we open a wall and find mold that's significantly more extensive than what was visible, we stop and show you before any additional work proceeds. You approve any scope change before we continue.

Do you offer financing?

We don't offer in-house financing. For larger remediation jobs, we can work with you on timing and phasing where the structure of the work allows. Call us to discuss your specific situation.

Does insurance cover mold removal?

Mold from a covered water event (burst pipe, storm damage) is often covered by homeowners insurance. Mold from slow leaks, poor ventilation, or deferred maintenance is typically not covered. We provide full documentation of cause and scope to support your claim when applicable.
