The Process

How Professional Mold Remediation
Works — Start to Finish

Legitimate remediation follows a defined process. Here's what it looks like from your first call to the clearance test.

1

Inspection & Written Scope

A certified technician visits, inspects visible mold, identifies moisture sources, and gives you a written scope with specific areas, inclusions, and total cost. No work starts before you approve.

2

Containment

The work area is isolated using plastic sheeting and negative air pressure to prevent spores from spreading to unaffected areas. Non-negotiable for any job larger than a small surface.

3

Material Removal

Porous materials — drywall, insulation — that have absorbed mold are removed and disposed of properly. Non-porous surfaces are HEPA-vacuumed and treated with antimicrobial solution.

4

Moisture Source Addressed

We fix or direct you to fix the moisture source before reconstruction. Skipping this step guarantees recurrence — it's where most contractors cut corners.

5

Clearance Testing

Post-remediation air sampling — ideally by a third party — verifies spore counts are within normal limits. This is your proof the job is done. We help arrange independent testing.

For very small, isolated mold on non-porous surfaces, cleaning is sometimes appropriate. For mold on drywall, wood, or any porous material — or any area over 10 square feet — professional remediation is the right call. Bleach doesn't penetrate porous materials; it cleans the surface while the colony continues below.

For small, well-contained jobs you can usually stay home with normal precautions. For larger jobs involving significant containment and air scrubbing, staying out of the work area is strongly recommended. We'll advise specifically based on your job scope.
