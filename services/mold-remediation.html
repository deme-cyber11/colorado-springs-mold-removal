Colorado Springs Mold Remediation — Done Right, Clearance Tested

Mold remediation isn't just cleaning visible mold off a surface. It's containment, systematic removal, moisture source correction, and post-remediation testing to confirm the job is actually done.

When You Need Mold Remediation

Not every mold situation requires professional remediation — a tiny patch of surface mildew in a well-ventilated bathroom is different from an established mold colony in a finished basement wall. Here's what requires professional intervention:

  • Mold covering more than 10 square feet — the EPA threshold where DIY stops being appropriate
  • Mold in HVAC systems, ductwork, or air handlers — any disturbance spreads spores building-wide
  • Mold inside wall cavities, under flooring, or in structural materials
  • Black or dark green mold — visible Stachybotrys requires full containment protocol
  • Mold that keeps returning after you've cleaned it — the moisture source hasn't been found
  • Mold following any water damage event, even if the visible water is long gone

Colorado Springs' humidity swings — dry winters followed by monsoon moisture — push water into basements and crawl spaces that never fully dry. By the time you see or smell it, the colony is often larger than what's visible.

How Mold Remediation Works

Our process follows certified remediation protocols — containment first, removal second, clearance last:

  1. Free Inspection: A certified technician assesses the affected area, identifies moisture sources, and provides a written scope and price before any work starts.
  2. Containment: We seal off the work area with plastic sheeting and establish negative air pressure before disturbing anything. Spores stay contained — they don't travel through your HVAC into other rooms.
  3. Remove and Treat: Contaminated porous materials (drywall, insulation, wood trim) are removed and properly disposed of. Non-porous surfaces are HEPA-vacuumed and treated with EPA-registered antimicrobial solution.
  4. Moisture Source Corrected: Remediation that doesn't address the moisture source fails within months. We address the source directly or give you clear direction on who needs to before rebuild begins.
  5. HEPA Air Scrubbing: Commercial air scrubbers run in the containment zone to capture airborne spores before containment is removed.
  6. Clearance Testing: Third-party air sampling after remediation verifies spore counts are within normal limits. You get a written clearance — proof the job is done.

What Every Remediation Job Includes

  • Written, itemized scope and price before work begins
  • Full containment with negative air pressure
  • HEPA vacuuming and EPA-registered antimicrobial treatment
  • Proper disposal of contaminated porous materials
  • Moisture source identification and correction guidance
  • Post-remediation clearance testing recommendation with third-party lab
  • Written warranty on remediated areas

What Does Mold Remediation Cost?

Remediation cost depends primarily on the size and location of the affected area. Realistic ranges for the Colorado Springs market:

  • Small area (under 10 sq ft): $500–$1,500
  • Mid-size (10–100 sq ft): $1,500–$4,000
  • Large / whole-room or basement: $4,000–$9,000

Most single-room or basement jobs land between $1,500–$3,500. The free inspection gives you the exact number for your situation — these ranges just set expectations before you call.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does mold remediation take?

Small to mid-size jobs: one day. Larger jobs involving multiple areas or significant material removal: 2–4 days. We provide a specific timeline in your written estimate so there are no surprises.

Do I need to leave my home during remediation?

For most small to medium projects, you don't need to vacate — avoid the work area and you're fine. For large-scale projects with significant Stachybotrys or multi-room contamination, we'll advise temporary relocation for the duration based on scope.

What is clearance testing and do I need it?

Clearance testing is independent air sampling after remediation is complete to confirm spore counts are back to normal levels. It's the only objective proof the job is done. We recommend it on every project above $2,000 and require third-party testing — not our own lab — so results are unbiased.

Is this covered by homeowners insurance?

Mold from a covered water event (burst pipe, storm damage) is often covered. Mold from slow leaks or deferred maintenance typically is not. We can document scope and cause to support an insurance claim when applicable.
