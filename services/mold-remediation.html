What Is Mold Remediation — and Why Does It Matter?

Mold remediation is not simply spraying bleach on a surface. True remediation follows the IICRC S520 Standard — a comprehensive protocol that addresses containment, physical removal, HEPA filtration, antimicrobial treatment, and verification testing. Anything less risks cross-contaminating other areas of your home and allowing mold to return within weeks.

At Springs Mold Solutions, every remediation project follows IICRC S520 protocols. We treat the cause as well as the symptom: mold growth is always driven by a moisture source, and unless that source is identified and fixed before or during remediation, mold will return. We won't remediate without first confirming the moisture problem has been addressed.

Our Mold Remediation Process

  1. Containment: We isolate the affected area with 6-mil poly sheeting and negative air pressure systems to prevent mold spores from spreading to unaffected parts of your home during the removal process. Occupants are protected.
  2. HEPA Air Filtration: Industrial HEPA air scrubbers run continuously throughout remediation, capturing airborne mold spores at the source. Air changes per hour are calculated for the affected space to ensure full filtration.
  3. Physical Removal: All mold-affected porous materials — drywall, insulation, subflooring — are safely removed and double-bagged in 6-mil poly for disposal. Non-porous materials are HEPA vacuumed and cleaned.
  4. Antimicrobial Treatment: All remaining surfaces in the containment zone are treated with EPA-registered antimicrobial products that kill residual mold and inhibit regrowth.
  5. HEPA Vacuum & Final Clean: All surfaces are HEPA vacuumed and wiped clean before containment is removed.
  6. Clearance Testing: Independent air sampling is conducted after remediation and sent to an AIHA-accredited lab. We don't consider the job done until clearance is confirmed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does mold remediation take in Colorado Springs?

Most residential projects take 1–5 days. A small bathroom or isolated wall cavity can be completed in one day. Basement or multi-room projects typically take 2–4 days. We provide a detailed timeline in your estimate.

Do I need to leave my home during mold remediation?

For most small to medium projects you don't need to leave the entire home — but you should avoid the work area. For large-scale projects involving black mold, HVAC contamination, or multi-room remediation, we recommend temporary relocation during active work.

What is clearance testing after mold remediation?

Clearance testing is post-remediation air sampling sent to an AIHA-accredited lab to verify mold levels have returned to normal. We use independent third-party labs. Clearance documentation is often required by insurance and is valuable for future home sales.
