Why a Professional Mold Inspection Matters in Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs has a reputation for dry air — but that doesn't mean your home is immune to mold. The Pikes Peak region's freeze-thaw cycling, snowmelt, and seasonal humidity fluctuations create localized wet conditions in basements, crawlspaces, and around windows where mold quietly establishes itself long before it becomes visible. By the time you smell musty air or see dark spots, mold growth is often already significant.

A professional mold inspection from Springs Mold Solutions goes far beyond looking at what you can see. We use moisture meters, infrared-assisted detection, and accredited air quality sampling to find mold where it's hiding — in wall cavities, under flooring, in HVAC systems, and in crawlspaces that most homeowners never enter.

What Our Inspection Includes

  1. Visual Inspection: A thorough walk-through of all accessible areas — attic, basement, crawlspace, bathrooms, kitchen, laundry room, and HVAC system. We document all visible mold, water staining, and signs of moisture intrusion.
  2. Moisture Mapping: Calibrated moisture meters identify elevated moisture levels in walls, floors, and ceilings — often revealing hidden mold growth or active leaks before damage is visible to the eye.
  3. Air Quality Testing: Surface or air samples collected from suspected problem areas and sent to an AIHA-accredited laboratory. Lab results identify mold species, spore counts, and comparative outdoor baseline levels.
  4. Source Identification: We locate the moisture source driving mold growth — without fixing the source, mold will return regardless of remediation. Colorado Springs-specific sources include snowmelt infiltration, HVAC condensate, and crawlspace vapor issues.
  5. Written Report: A detailed inspection report with findings, photos, moisture readings, lab results (if applicable), and prioritized recommendations delivered within 48 hours.

When Should You Get a Mold Inspection?

  • You smell musty or earthy odors in your home, especially in the basement
  • You've had a plumbing leak, roof leak, or flooding — even if it was "cleaned up"
  • Family members have unexplained allergy symptoms, headaches, or respiratory issues
  • You're buying or selling a home in Colorado Springs
  • You have visible dark spots on walls, ceilings, or grout that keep coming back
  • Your home sat vacant or unheated during a Colorado winter

Colorado Springs Mold Inspection Cost

Visual mold inspections are free. If air quality testing or lab analysis is recommended, costs run $150–$400 depending on the number of samples. A full inspection with lab analysis typically runs $250–$500. Remediation quotes are always provided at no charge after the inspection.
