Colorado Springs Mold Inspection — Know What You're Dealing With

Mold inspection is the first step every homeowner should take when they suspect a problem. Without accurate detection, you're either over-treating a small issue or missing a large one that will cost far more to fix later.

Home › Services › Mold Inspection

When You Need a Mold Inspection

Most mold problems in the Pikes Peak region go undetected for months. The dry air here creates a false sense of security — homeowners assume moisture can't be an issue when it hasn't rained in weeks. But Colorado Springs' rapid humidity swings — bone-dry stretches followed by monsoon downpours — push moisture into basements and crawl spaces where it lingers unseen.

You need an inspection if any of these apply:

  • Musty or earthy odors in the basement, crawl space, or near HVAC returns
  • A plumbing leak, roof leak, or flood event — even one that was "cleaned up"
  • Family members with persistent allergy symptoms, headaches, or respiratory issues indoors
  • Visible dark spots on walls, ceilings, or grout that keep returning after cleaning
  • A home purchase or sale — mold that standard inspections miss can become a major liability
  • Condensation on windows or walls that persists through winter

If you wait until mold is extensive and visible, remediation costs climb significantly. An inspection now is the cheapest decision you can make.

How Mold Inspection Works

Our inspection follows a structured process designed to identify mold where it's actually hiding — not just what's visible on surfaces:

  1. Visual Assessment: A thorough walk-through of all accessible areas — attic, basement, crawlspace, bathrooms, kitchen, laundry room, and HVAC system. We document visible mold, water staining, and moisture intrusion signs with photos.
  2. Moisture Mapping: Calibrated moisture meters scan walls, floors, and ceilings for elevated readings that indicate hidden moisture — often revealing active leaks or condensation problems before mold becomes visible.
  3. Source Identification: We locate the moisture source driving any mold we find. Snowmelt infiltration, condensate drain issues, exhaust fans venting into attics, and crawlspace vapor migration are the most common culprits in this area.
  4. Air Quality Sampling (if warranted): Surface swabs or air cassettes collected from suspected problem areas and sent to an AIHA-accredited lab. Results identify mold species, spore counts, and comparison to outdoor baseline levels.
  5. Written Report: Findings, photos, moisture readings, lab results (if applicable), and prioritized recommendations — delivered within 48 hours of the inspection.

What Every Inspection Includes

  • Full visual walk-through of all accessible areas
  • Calibrated moisture meter readings across suspect areas
  • Identification of moisture source(s) driving any mold found
  • Written findings report with photos
  • Honest, itemized remediation quote — no obligation to proceed
  • Air or surface sampling available at additional cost when lab confirmation is needed

What Does Mold Inspection Cost?

Visual mold inspections are free. If air quality testing or lab analysis is recommended, costs run $150–$400 depending on the number of samples taken. A full inspection with lab report typically runs $300–$600. Remediation quotes are always provided at no charge following the inspection. You'll know exactly what you're dealing with, what it'll cost to fix, and what the process looks like — before any work begins.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a professional mold inspection include?

Our inspection covers visual assessment of all accessible areas, moisture meter readings, identification of water intrusion sources, optional lab-analyzed air or surface sampling, and a written report with findings and recommendations. You get a complete picture, not just "we found some mold."

How long does a mold inspection take?

Most residential inspections take 1–2 hours depending on the size of the home. Larger homes or those with complex crawlspaces and finished basements may take 2–3 hours. Lab results, when ordered, return in 3–5 business days.

Do I need to be home for the inspection?

Yes. We need access to all areas of the home including attic access, basement, crawlspace entry, and utility spaces. Having you present also lets us walk through findings in real time and answer questions on the spot.

Is mold inspection covered by homeowners insurance?

Typically not on its own. If mold resulted from a covered water event (burst pipe, storm damage), some policies cover inspection and remediation. Mold from gradual moisture accumulation or deferred maintenance is generally excluded. We can help document cause and scope for an insurance claim if applicable.
