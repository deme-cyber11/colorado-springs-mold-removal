Found Mold? Here's What You Need to Know First.

Falcon's rural-suburban character — newer homes on larger lots east of Colorado Springs — creates mold conditions that differ from urban neighborhoods. Crawl space foundations are common, properties are more spread out, and many homes rely on private wells. These factors combine with the area's dramatic humidity swings to create ideal conditions for mold that goes undetected until it's well-established.

Mold is a solvable problem. The key is finding all of it, containing the work area correctly, removing what needs to go, and testing to confirm it's done. We follow certified protocols on every job — from the first free inspection through final clearance testing.

Why Falcon Homes Are Vulnerable

Crawl space foundations with inadequate vapor barriers are the most common mold source we find in Falcon. Many homes were built in the 2000s with minimal vapor barriers that have since degraded. Soil moisture evaporates continuously onto cold wood joists, creating mold conditions that accumulate for years before showing up as odors or visible growth.

Larger lots mean more drainage variability. Properties with acreage have more surface area for water to pool around foundations. After monsoon events, low spots near foundations create sustained moisture pressure that infiltrates basements and crawl spaces — a problem less common on smaller urban lots with more controlled drainage.

Well water systems can hide slow leaks longer. Properties on private wells don't have the same pressure monitoring as municipal connections. Slow leaks at pressure tanks, supply lines, and water heater connections can run for months — creating the sustained moisture that enables hidden mold growth in walls and floors.

Without containment, spores spread fast in open floor plans. Falcon's newer construction often features open floor plans with fewer interior walls to limit spore migration. Disturbing mold without proper negative pressure containment affects the entire living area quickly.

Quick Answers Before You Call

How do I know if my crawl space has mold?

Musty odors from floor registers or the lower level of the home are the most common signal. Other signs: unexplained worsening of indoor allergies, soft spots in flooring above the crawl space, or visible dark growth on joists visible from the access hatch. Most Falcon homeowners don't enter their crawl space regularly — a professional inspection is the only reliable way to know.

Is crawl space encapsulation worth it in Falcon?

For any Falcon crawl space that's had mold, encapsulation is the permanent solution. It seals soil vapor migration — the root cause of crawl space mold — and prevents recurrence. Most homeowners do both remediation and encapsulation at the same visit since the space is already open.
