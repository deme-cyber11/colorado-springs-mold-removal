Falcon, CO Mold Removal

Falcon is one of the fastest-growing areas east of Colorado Springs, with master-planned communities like Meridian Ranch, Banning Lewis Ranch, and Indigo Ranch expanding rapidly across the prairie. While the area feels dry and open, Falcon homes face their own set of mold challenges — many of them related to the region's unique soil conditions and the rapid pace of new construction.

Why Falcon Homes Get Mold

  • Bentonite clay soil — Falcon sits on some of the most expansive clay in El Paso County, causing foundation movement and cracks that admit moisture
  • Well water systems — Many Falcon properties rely on wells; pressure tank failures and pipe condensation create hidden moisture
  • New construction moisture — Concrete, drywall, and paint in new homes release moisture for 12-18 months; combined with a sealed-up house, this feeds mold
  • Septic system proximity — Properties on septic systems can have elevated soil moisture near drain fields
  • Wind-driven rain — Falcon's exposed prairie location means storms drive rain horizontally into exterior walls and around window seals

New Construction Mold

We get a lot of calls from Falcon homeowners who bought new construction and found mold within the first year or two. This isn't uncommon — new homes release significant moisture from curing concrete and drying materials, and if the HVAC system isn't properly balanced or the home was closed up during construction with inadequate dehumidification, mold can establish quickly in wall cavities, closets, and HVAC systems.
