Woodland Park Mold Remediation

At 8,500 feet above sea level, Woodland Park — the "City Above the Clouds" — offers mountain living at its best. But the same elevation, snowpack, and forested terrain that make it beautiful also create persistent moisture challenges for homeowners. Log cabins, A-frames, and mountain homes along the Ute Pass corridor are particularly susceptible to mold due to their construction and environment.

Mountain Home Mold Risks

  • Snow load moisture — Heavy winter snowpack on roofs and against walls introduces sustained moisture as it melts
  • Log home vulnerabilities — Settling logs create gaps where moisture enters; chinking and caulking failures are common
  • Seasonal vacancy — Vacation homes and cabins sit closed during winter, trapping humidity without ventilation
  • Poor drainage in forest soil — Pine needle duff and forest soil retain moisture against foundations
  • Ice dam damage — Ice dams on roofs force water under shingles and into attic spaces

Services for Woodland Park Properties

  • Free mold inspection — thorough assessment of mountain homes
  • Crawl space remediation — critical for mountain homes with exposed crawl spaces
  • Full mold remediation — log homes, cabins, and modern construction
  • Water damage restoration — ice dam and pipe freeze recovery

Serving the Ute Pass Corridor

We serve all of Woodland Park, including the neighborhoods along Rampart Range Road, the Talon Pointe and Shiloh Pines areas, and the mountain communities along Highway 24. We also cover Divide, Florissant, and the Ute Pass communities between Manitou Springs and Woodland Park — including Green Mountain Falls, Cascade, and Chipita Park.
