Castle Rock is the county seat of Douglas County, situated along I-25 midway between Denver and Colorado Springs. With a population of approximately 78,000, Castle Rock has experienced explosive growth over the past two decades, transforming from a small ranching town into one of Colorado's most desirable suburban communities. The town is named after the prominent castle-shaped butte at its center, and neighborhoods like The Meadows, Crystal Valley, Founders Village, and Plum Creek feature newer construction with finished basements and modern floor plans. However, Castle Rock's expansive clay soils present a persistent moisture challenge. These soils swell when wet and shrink when dry, creating pressure against foundations and opening pathways for water intrusion. Even recently built Castle Rock homes develop mold when moisture migrates through concrete basement walls or accumulates in poorly ventilated crawl spaces. Frequently Asked Questions — Mold Removal in Castle Rock

Why do newer Castle Rock homes develop mold problems?

Castle Rock has been one of Colorado's fastest-growing towns, with its population nearly doubling over the past 15 years. Rapid construction sometimes means homes are built on lots before soil has fully settled, and the area's expansive clay soils shift and retain moisture against foundations. Many newer Castle Rock developments in Meadows, Crystal Valley, and Plum Creek feature finished basements that trap humidity behind drywall. When combined with Colorado's freeze-thaw cycles pushing moisture through concrete, even homes built in the last five years can develop hidden mold in basement walls, crawl spaces, and around window wells.
